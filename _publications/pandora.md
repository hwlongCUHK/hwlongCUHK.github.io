This paper presents a comprehensive technical and graphical analysis of the ERC-404 and DN-404 blockchain standards, which integrate fungible (ERC-20) and non-fungible (ERC-721) token features. We examine the smart contracts behind these standards to understand their operational mechanisms and market impact. Our findings highlight challenges such as high transaction costs and network congestion in ERC-404, while DN-404 provides an evolution that reduces complexities and costs. We perform a detailed graph analysis of Fungible Token Transfer, Non-Fungible Token Mint, and Burn Graphs, revealing a power-law distribution that suggests concentration of activities among few addresses.
