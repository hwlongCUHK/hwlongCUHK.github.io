This paper addresses the volatility of transaction fees in consortium blockchains, which rely on fees as the primary mining incentive. We propose a solution using Dynamic Transaction Storage (DTS) strategies with a Verkle Tree-based storage mechanism to stabilize block incentives. By optimizing DTS with algorithms like PSO and GBO, we identify a time-based transaction prioritization strategy that minimizes fee volatility and reduces network bandwidth. Our approach provides a scalable, efficient solution to mitigate deviant mining behaviors and ensure stable mining incentives in consortium blockchain environments.
